# Example: Product Manager Feature Bet

## Input

A product manager is considering a new feature:

"Add an AI-generated account summary to the CRM homepage so sales reps can prepare for calls faster."

## B2B Idea Vetting Brief: AI Account Summary

## Decision Under Review

Should the product team prioritize an AI-generated account summary on the CRM homepage for sales reps?

## Strongest Case

The feature targets a frequent, high-friction workflow: pre-call preparation. If reps already jump between notes, CRM fields, call recordings, and Slack threads, a concise account summary could save time and improve call quality.

## Weakest Assumption

The summary may not be trusted at the moment of use. If reps need to verify every claim before a call, the feature becomes another surface to check instead of a preparation shortcut.

## Evidence Gaps

- How reps currently prepare for calls and which sources they trust most.
- Whether inaccurate or stale summaries would create material risk.
- Whether managers or reps care more about speed, completeness, or confidence.

## Recommended Next Move

Test first.

The workflow is real, but the product bet depends on trust and source quality. Prioritizing a full homepage feature before proving that reps rely on the summary would create avoidable product risk.

## One-Week Test

Create 10 manual account summaries from existing CRM data for upcoming calls. Give them to 5 reps before real calls and ask them to mark what they used, what they checked, and what they ignored.

## Confidence Level

Medium — the job is concrete, but the critical assumption is whether the generated summary is trusted enough to change behavior.
