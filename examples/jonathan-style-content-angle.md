# Example: Jonathan-Style B2B Content Angle

## Input

A freelance PMM and B2B content writer is considering a new article angle:

"Most AI content fails because teams treat it like cheaper writing instead of a new editorial operating model."

## B2B Idea Vetting Brief: AI Editorial Operating Model

## Decision Under Review

Should a freelance PMM/content consultant invest in turning "AI content needs an editorial operating model, not cheaper writing" into a public point of view and client-facing offer?

## Strongest Case

The angle names a real market tension. B2B teams are experimenting with AI content, but many still judge the work through the old lens of output volume. A consultant who can reframe the problem as editorial operations can speak to a higher-value buyer than "content production" alone.

## Weakest Assumption

The buyer may agree with the critique but not feel enough urgency to pay for the operating model. The idea could earn nods from other content people without creating budget-holder action.

## Evidence Gaps

- Whether marketing leaders are already asking for AI content governance or workflow help.
- Whether the phrase "editorial operating model" is legible to the intended buyer.
- Whether this can become a service package, not just a smart post.

## Recommended Next Move

Test first.

The underlying insight is strong enough to test, but not yet specific enough to package. The next signal should come from buyer behavior: replies, calls, or requests for help from people who own content quality and budget.

## One-Week Test

Send a 300-word version of the argument to 10 B2B marketing leaders or content leads and ask: "Where does this match what you are seeing, and what would you need help changing?" Track whether anyone names a workflow, budget, or internal pressure without being prompted.

## Confidence Level

Medium — the problem is timely and plausible, but the willingness-to-pay signal is still unproven.
