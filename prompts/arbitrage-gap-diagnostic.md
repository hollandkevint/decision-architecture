# Arbitrage Gap Diagnostic

3 questions for evaluating any market, industry, or business model. Based on Nate Jones' Arbitrage Gap Framework.

## The Prompt

```
I'm going to describe an industry, market, or business model. Evaluate it by answering these 3 questions:

1. What inefficiency is this built on? Name the specific gap — information asymmetry, speed gap, fragmentation, discipline gap, judgment gap, relationship gap, or system-level thinking gap.

2. How fast can AI close this gap? Rate as Fast (1-2 years), Medium (3-5 years), or Slow (5+ years). Explain why.

3. What new gap does the closure create? When this gap closes, what becomes the new bottleneck? Who owns that bottleneck?

Then tell me:
- Am I positioned in a closing gap or a durable one?
- What's one specific action I should take based on this analysis?
```

## The Key Insight

Access to AI is universal. Advantage is not. 7.6% of organizations have rebuilt their processes around what AI makes possible. 92.4% bolted AI onto existing processes. Systems compound; pilots don't.

The durable gaps are structural: judgment, taste, relationships, system-level thinking. The closing gaps are informational: research, synthesis, aggregation. Decision architecture operates in structural gap territory.

## When to Use

- Evaluating where to position a new product or business
- Assessing career risk (is your value in a closing or durable gap?)
- Analyzing competitors (what gap are they exploiting? is it closing?)
- Strategic planning (where should the team invest?)
