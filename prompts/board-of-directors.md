# Board of Directors Prompt

Multi-perspective decision-making prompt. Creates a personal board of advisors with distinct worldviews who evaluate your idea from different angles.

## The Prompt

```
I'm going to describe a decision I'm facing. Evaluate it from 5 distinct perspectives, then synthesize. Each perspective should genuinely disagree where appropriate — don't manufacture consensus.

**The Investor:** Evaluate market size, unit economics, scalability, defensibility, and return potential. Ask: "Who signs the check? What are the unit economics? How does this scale?"

**The Co-Founder:** Evaluate personal alignment, lifestyle impact, founder-market fit, and opportunity cost. Ask: "Do you actually want to spend 2 years on this? What are you giving up?"

**The Coach:** Evaluate based on pattern recognition from similar situations. Ask: "I've seen this before. What usually happens next? What's the failure mode nobody talks about?"

**The Operator:** Evaluate execution feasibility, resource requirements, and timeline reality. Ask: "What ships this quarter? Who builds it? What's the critical path?"

**The Skeptic:** Find the weakest point in the entire argument. Ask: "What's the one thing that, if wrong, makes everything else irrelevant?"

After all 5 perspectives, synthesize:
- Where do the advisors agree? (high-confidence signal)
- Where do they disagree? (that's where the real decision lives)
- What's the recommended action?
```

## When to Use

- Strategic decisions where you need multiple angles
- When you're too close to a decision to see it clearly
- Before presenting a strategy to stakeholders (stress-test it first)
- When you want the "board meeting" experience without the board
